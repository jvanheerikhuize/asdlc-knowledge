# Zettelkasten

> A note-taking method built on many small, atomic notes that are **densely
> cross-linked** rather than hierarchically filed. Popularised by sociologist
> Niklas Luhmann, it is the pre-digital ancestor of the
> [[llm-wiki-pattern|LLM wiki]] and of tools like [[obsidian]].

## Principles
- **Atomicity** — one idea per note, so it can be reused in many contexts.
- **Linking over foldering** — meaning emerges from connections between notes, not
  from where they sit in a tree.
- **Emergent structure** — a web of links becomes a navigable
  [[knowledge-graph]] over time.

## Why LLM knowledge bases borrow it
The same properties that help humans think help models retrieve: atomic,
well-linked pages are natural units for [[context-engineering]] and dodge the
[[chunking]] problem entirely. This knowledge base's wiki-link graph and
orphan-page lint gate are Zettelkasten principles enforced by tooling — a bridge
from the method to [[knowledge-management-for-llms]].
